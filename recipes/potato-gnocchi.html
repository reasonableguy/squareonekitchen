Ingredients

  • 1.4kg washed russet potatoes
  • 100g AP flour (plus more for dusting)
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 5g butter
  • 1 & 1/4 cup milk + extra for brushing

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 450Â°F.
  2. Puncture the potatoes all over with a fork or knife, so that the steam can escape, and place them on the wire rack.
  3. Bake the potatoes in the middle of the oven for 45 minutes.
  4. Remove the potatoes from the oven. Cut the hot potatoes in half and scoop the steaming flesh out into a bowl.
  5. Run the potato flesh through a ricer or push it through a colander.
  6. Spread the riced potato on the counter to cool (around 5-10 minutes).
  7. Blend the egg yolks and drizzle them over the cooled potato.
  8. Sift about 2/3 of your flour (around 70g) over the potato.
  9. Using a scraper, chop the mixture together.
  10. Flatten your ball of dough and dust with the remaining flour.
  11. Continue to fold and press the dough until you achieve good consistency.
  12. Roll the dough into a log.
  13. Clean a space on your counter and dust it with flour.
  14. Cut a 1/2-inch slice off the log and roll it under the heel of your hand until it forms a 1/2-inch thick sausage.
  15. Repeat with the rest of the dough.
  16. Cut your sausages into gnocchi-sized pillows. Dusting with flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  17. (Optional) roll your gnocchi on a rigagnocchi board or a fork to create sauce-grabbing ridges.
  18. Cook the gnocchi straight away in salted boiling water. Cook in batches. When it floats to the surfaces it's cooked.
  19. Eat straight away or spread them out on a tray and freeze for later (reheat them by repeating step 19).
