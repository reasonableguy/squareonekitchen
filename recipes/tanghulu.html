Ingredients

  • 250g fruit
  • ice
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup white sugar

Method

  1. Prepare the Fruit: Wash and dry your fruit thoroughly. Skewer 1-3 pieces of fruit onto each wooden stick.
  2. Make the Sugar Syrup: In a medium pot, combine sugar and water. Heat over medium heat until it reaches 275-300°F. If you don't have a thermometer, test by dipping a spoon or skewer in the syrup and then in ice water—if it hardens, it's ready.
  3. Dip the Fruit: Once the sugar syrup is ready, reduce the heat to low. Carefully dip the skewered fruits into the syrup, rotating to coat evenly.
  4. Harden the Coating: Let the excess syrup drip off the fruit, then dip the skewers into ice water for 2-4 seconds to harden the sugar coating.
  5. Serve Immediately: Enjoy your tanghulu while the sugar coating is still crisp.
  6. Notes: If the sugar mixture crystallizes, add a little water and reheat to the right temperature. Avoid overheating to prevent making caramel instead of a hard sugar coating.
