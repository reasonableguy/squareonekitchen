Prep time:

Cook time:

Total time:

Ingredients

    Cookie Ingredients
  • 115g unsalted butter
  • 100g sugar
  • 55g golden syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 165g all-purpose flour
  • 35g cocoa powder, plus more for dusting

    • Filling Ingredients
  • 170g unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 240g powdered sugar

Method

  • Preheat oven to 350°F with the rack in the middle.
Cookie Dough:
  1. Combine first five cookie ingredients (i.e. not flour and cocoa) using a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment.
  2. Mix on low speed to moisten all the ingredients.Increase speed and beat until fluffy (~5 mins). Scrape bowl as needed).
  3. WHile the butter mix is going, sift your flour and cocoa together.
  4. Turn the mixer to slow and slowly add the flour-cocoa mix to the butter.
  5. Keep beating until you get a smooth dough.
  6. Turn the dough out onto the counter and knead into a smooth ball.
  7. Cut the ball in two and form two patties.
  8. dust the counter, the first pattie and your rollling pin.
  9. Roll dough to around 1/8 inch thick.
  10. cut into 1 1/2–inch round discs with a cookie cutter.
  11. Repeat with remaining dough.
  12. Place your discs on an oven tray about 1 inch apart.
  13. Bake until firm and dry (about 15 mins).
  14. Remove from oven and cool to room temperature.
  15. When cooled, flip half of the discs upside-down to be the bottom of your oreos.
Filling:
  1. In a medium pot, slowly melt butter over medium-low heat.
  2. Continue to simmer (turning the heat down if brown bits start to form) until butter stops hissing and falls silent.
  3. Strain into a mixing bowl.
  4. Add vanilla, salt, and powdered sugar.
  5. Mix on low to moisten, then increase to medium until creamy.
  6. scrape filling into a pastry bag with a 1/2-inch nozzle.
  7. Add around 1 Tbsp of filling to each upside-down disc.
  8. Squish a lid on top to make teh sandwich.
  9. Share with people you like.
