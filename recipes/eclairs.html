Prep time:

Cook time:

Total time:

Ingredients

    For the Dough

  • 1 cup water
  • 60g butter
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup flour
  • 4 large eggs

    • For the Crème Pâtissière Filling

  • 2 cups milk
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 egg
  • ⅓ cup white sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    • For the Chocolate Glaze

  • 1/2 cup cream
  • 100-150g chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon butter (optional)

Method

    For the Éclairs

  1. In a small saucepan, heat water, butter, salt, and sugar until boiling.
  2. Lower the heat, add flour all at once, and stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until a ball of dough forms and detaches from the pan sides.
  3. Transfer dough to a bowl to cool briefly. Then, add eggs one by one, beating well after each addition to fully incorporate. Start with a fork, then switch to an electric mixer to prevent clumping.
  4. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Prepare a baking sheet by greasing and flouring it.
  5. Fill a pastry bag with the choux pastry dough, using a tip with about a 1" slit for best results.
  6. Pipe the dough onto the prepared baking sheet in 3" logs, spacing them to allow for expansion.
  7. Bake for 15 minutes, rotating the sheet halfway. Then, lower the temperature to 375°F (190°C) and bake for another 20 minutes, rotating again halfway, until golden and puffed. Let them cool at room temperature.

    8. For the Crème Pâtissière Filling

  8. In a saucepan, heat 2 cups of milk with ¼ cup sugar until it boils.
  9. In a bowl, whisk 2 egg yolks and 1 whole egg together. In another bowl, mix ⅓ cup sugar with ¼ cup cornstarch, then blend this into the eggs.
  10. Gradually add the boiled milk to the egg mixture, stirring constantly.
  11. Return this mixture to the saucepan. Heat it slowly, stirring all the time, until it thickens.
  12. Take off the heat, then stir in 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.
  13. Strain through a sieve to remove lumps and cover the surface with plastic wrap to avoid skin formation and chill before using.

    14. For the Chocolate Glaze

  14. Pour the cream into a small saucepan and heat until small bubbles form around the edge.
  15. Add the chocolate to the cream and let sit for a couple of minutes to melt.
  16. Stir vigorously (you can add a tablespoon of butter for a shinier glaze).
  17. Stir it up till it's all nice and smooth.
  18. Let it cool down till it's thick enough to slather on (It takes a couple of hours to get there... and yeah, we might've jumped the gun a bit on that part).

    19. To Assemble

  19. Split the éclairs in half and fill the bottom half with about 2 tablespoons of the pastry cream.
  20. Either dip the top in the glaze, or place it on the bottom half and drizzle the glaze over the top.
