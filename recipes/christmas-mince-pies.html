Ingredients

    Filling

  • 2 cups of mixed dried fruit (such as raisins, currants, sultanas, and chopped dates)
  • 1/2 cup of chopped candied peel
  • 1 small cooking apple, peeled, cored, and finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup of brandy, rum, or sherry (optional, can be replaced with orange juice for a non-alcoholic version)
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted

    • Pastry

  • 4 oz butter
  • 4 oz sugar
  • 8 oz flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 egg

Method

    Filling

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the dried fruits, candied peel, and chopped apple. Mix well.
  2. Stir in the brown sugar, orange zest, lemon zest, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and ginger. Mix thoroughly to ensure even distribution of the spices.
  3. Add the orange juice, lemon juice, melted butter, and your choice of alcohol (or additional orange juice if you're making it non-alcoholic). Mix well.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the mixture sit in the refrigerator. Ideally, let it marinate overnight, or at least for a few hours. This allows the flavors to meld together.
  5. While the filling is marinating, prepare your pie crusts.

    6. Pastry

  6. In a mixing bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until they are well combined and fluffy.
  7. Crack an egg into the mixture and beat it thoroughly until the egg is fully incorporated and the mixture is smooth.
  8. Sift the flour and baking powder directly into the bowl containing the creamed mixture. Mix these ingredients well to form a consistent dough.
  9. Roll the dough into a ball and wrap it in cling film. Place it in the refrigerator to harden, which will make it easier to roll out and cut into shapes later.

    10. Putting It All Together

  10. Preheat oven to 350Â°F.
  11. Fill your pie crusts with the mincemeat filling.
  12. Cover them with additional pastry in the form of a lid or lattice.
  13. Bake for 12 minutes.
  14. Allow the pies to cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.
